## Company Values

Use this reference to map SBI evidence blocks to the company values in Step 7. Each SBI block may map to one or more values. Apply the value whose definition best fits the observed behavior — do not stretch a weak fit.

## The Six Values

| Value | One-Line Definition |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Own It** | Takes full accountability for outcomes, follows through without needing to be chased, and treats problems as theirs to solve regardless of formal ownership. |
| **Dive Deep** | Goes beyond surface-level analysis to understand root causes, engages with technical or domain complexity, and produces work that reflects genuine depth of investigation. |
| **Deliver Value Fast** | Prioritises shipping working solutions over perfect ones, reduces cycle time, unblocks others, and keeps momentum on what matters most to customers or the business. |
| **Bring Good Vibes** | Elevates team energy and morale through consistent positive engagement, constructive framing under pressure, and behaviour that makes collaboration feel productive rather than draining. |
| **Raise the Bar** | Pushes standards higher — for themselves, for teammates, and for the work — by identifying gaps, proposing improvements, and modelling a higher level of craft or rigour. |
| **Stay Humble** | Seeks input before deciding, credits others, acknowledges uncertainty openly, and treats feedback as useful data rather than a threat. |

## Mapping Rules

- **Own It**: Look for evidence of self-initiated action, taking ownership of a problem without being asked, or seeing something through when it was easier to hand off.
- **Dive Deep**: Look for evidence of research, root-cause analysis, technical investigation, or going beyond the brief to understand something fully.
- **Deliver Value Fast**: Look for evidence of unblocking a dependency, shipping an increment, reducing time-to-resolution, or cutting scope deliberately to keep progress moving.
- **Bring Good Vibes**: Look for evidence of explicit encouragement, keeping tone constructive during incidents or conflict, or energising a flagging discussion.
- **Raise the Bar**: Look for evidence of proposing a better process, catching an error that would have shipped, or modelling a standard that others then adopted.
- **Stay Humble**: Look for evidence of seeking review, openly crediting a collaborator, changing position when presented with evidence, or naming uncertainty rather than bluffing.

## Worked Example

**SBI block**: During a P1 database incident in February 2026, Sarah Chen posted a detailed incident timeline in #incidents within 20 minutes of the alert, tagging the on-call team and providing three hypotheses ranked by likelihood. The incident was resolved 40 minutes faster than the team's historical average for similar events.

**Value mapping**:
- **Own It** — she initiated the timeline without being assigned to it
- **Dive Deep** — she produced ranked hypotheses rather than a single guess
- **Deliver Value Fast** — her structured update accelerated resolution time
