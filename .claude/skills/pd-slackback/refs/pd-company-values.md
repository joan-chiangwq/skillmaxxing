## Pandora Principles

Use this reference to map SBI evidence blocks to the Pandora Principles in Step 7. Each SBI block may map to one or more values. Apply the value whose sub-behaviors best fit the observed behavior — do not stretch a weak fit.

## Value Details

### Own It
- **Get Things Done:** Solves problems end to end and goes outside of scope when needed.
- **Think Long Term:** Takes decisions best for the business above personal interests.
- **Use Resources Carefully:** Operates with lean teams and is mindful about resource use.

**IC Exemplars:**
- IC3: Manages projects autonomously. Seeks frugal and efficient solutions. Takes responsibility for complex problems in their area.
- IC4: Proposes innovative solutions with significant impact. Manages resources with long-term impact. Prioritizes business over individual area interests.

### Dive Deep
- **Challenge the Data:** Goes beyond averages and is skeptical when metrics and anecdotes differ.
- **Know How Things Work:** Understands how things work.
- **Be Hands-On:** Rolls up sleeves and engages at every level.

**IC Exemplars:**
- IC3: Challenges data and seeks deep understanding. Collaborates comfortably from detail to broader level. Connects work impact to related areas.
- IC4: Researches internal and external issues. Specific and in-depth discipline knowledge. Involved in critical stages to ensure quality.

### Deliver Value Fast
- **Focus on What Matters:** Relentlessly prioritizes based on consumer, rider, and partner needs.
- **Move with Pace:** Drives impact through speed.
- **Embrace Change:** Adapts quickly and embraces change.

**IC Exemplars:**
- IC3: Quickly moves to implement solutions. Prioritizes based on ecosystem value. Iterates on existing solutions.
- IC4: Turns ideas into actionable solutions. Knows when to say 'no' to non-strategic goals. Fosters flexibility and improvement.

### Raise the Bar
- **Set a High Standard:** Sets ambitious goals.
- **Hire & Develop the Best:** Builds high-performing teams.
- **Provide Actionable Feedback:** Provides constructive feedback.

**IC Exemplars:**
- IC3: Collaborates effectively with other areas. Maintains resilient attitude, inspiring others. Celebrates team's achievements.
- IC4: Builds trusting relationships with stakeholders. Demonstrates 'can-do' attitude. Mediates conflicts and promotes positive environment.

### Bring Good Vibes
- **Be a Good Team Player:** Acts with empathy and considers impact on others.
- **Focus on What's Possible:** Focuses on what's possible even in tough times.
- **Create a Positive Workplace:** Creates a positive environment and celebrates each other's success.

**IC Exemplars:**
- IC3: Sets ambitious goals in their projects. Provides constructive feedback to peers. Shares deep expertise to build team.
- IC4: Generates innovative solutions that raise standards. Attracts and develops talent for initiatives. Provides feedback to colleagues and leaders for growth.

### Stay Humble
- **Open-Mindedness:** Has no ego and is open to challenging own opinions.
- **No Entitlement:** Does not take things for granted and rejects entitlement.
- **Continuous Improvement:** Never done learning and always seeks to improve.

**IC Exemplars:**
- IC3: Challenges own opinions. Analyzes mistakes in structured way. Promotes environment where all voices are heard.
- IC4: Challenges own solutions via diverse perspectives. Shares lessons learned from mistakes. Invites debate and challenge.

## Mapping Rules

- **Own It**: Look for evidence of self-initiated action, going beyond scope, seeing something through without being chased, or choosing the lean/frugal path.
- **Dive Deep**: Look for evidence of data skepticism, root-cause investigation, hands-on engagement, or connecting work impact to adjacent areas.
- **Deliver Value Fast**: Look for evidence of shipping an increment, unblocking a dependency, cutting scope deliberately to keep momentum, or adapting quickly to changing requirements.
- **Raise the Bar**: Look for evidence of setting ambitious goals, developing teammates, or delivering feedback that raised quality.
- **Bring Good Vibes**: Look for evidence of explicit encouragement, keeping tone constructive during incidents or conflict, or energising a flagging discussion.
- **Stay Humble**: Look for evidence of seeking review, openly crediting a collaborator, changing position when presented with evidence, or naming uncertainty rather than bluffing.

## Worked Example

**SBI block**: During a P1 database incident in February 2026, Sarah Chen posted a detailed incident timeline in #incidents within 20 minutes of the alert, tagging the on-call team and providing three hypotheses ranked by likelihood. The incident was resolved 40 minutes faster than the team's historical average for similar events.

**Value mapping**:
- **Own It** — she initiated the timeline without being assigned to it (Get Things Done)
- **Dive Deep** — she produced ranked hypotheses rather than a single guess (Challenge the Data, Be Hands-On)
- **Deliver Value Fast** — her structured update accelerated resolution time (Move with Pace)
